HISTORY OF Iligan City National High School

On July 1, 1963, Iligan City National High School was established by Mayor Camilo P. Cabili. There were 13 staff members and 264 students enrolled in first- and second-year high school classes when it initially opened. The city council gave a budget of P64,000.00 for the school's operations. Over the next twenty years, the school experienced a lot of development. The number of students had increased from 264 students in 1963 to 7,140 students in 1983, and the budget reached almost P4,000,000.00.

The logo of Iligan City National High School serves as a representation of the institution's core values, mission, and vision. It reflects the school's commitment to giving students the best possible education, providing a caring the environment, and helping them to become responsible citizens and lifelong learners. Surrounding the shield are the words "Iligan City National High School," openly showed to represent the school's identity and connection.
